Why you should up-skill and re-skill your employees

Did you know that Shell collaborates with Udacity to train their workers in artificial intelligence? Why are they doing this? Well, according the to the CEO of Udacity, Gabe Dalporto, Shell realized that there was mismatch between the number of AI-related projects it was working on and the number of data scientists needed to deliver on them. And the worst thing a company can do is to fire employees and hire new ones – because reskilling your employees is not just faster, but it is also cheaper.

In the past 4 years we at Codecool helped more than a 100 companies to upskill or reskill their workforce, we learned a lot about the relationship between the company and its workforce. We saw firsthand how digital skills help to grow any company’s bottom-line – as employees become more fluent in digital technologies they become more productive and their contribution to your profit increases.

We also saw that almost every company has a hidden talent stream – their employees! You probably have not yet realized that with the right upskilling program, you can unlock the true potential in your workforce. Which in turn will lead to shorter and cheaper hiring processes, better training results and talent retention. And when you add these factors together, it will make your company more competitive and resilient.

What to expect in the CEE region?

“After looking at the market it is clear that more and more companies are realizing that it is much more efficient and cost effective to reskill or upskill employees who already know the company, its processes, products and services than to look for new employees on the job market, interview them and train them.” – says Lea Kalocsai, head of sales at Codecool.

Codecool has years of experience in helping corporations keeping up with the demands of the fast moving digital economy. Our mentors helped the up- and reskilling of employees in the fields of business intelligence, big data, Java and agile methodologies.

One of our most complex project was a re- and upskilling of a telecommunications provider’s value management team. The team consisted of diverse professionals from the fields of programming, law, marketing and finance. They wanted this team to operate as a purely data-driven unit. With our training and the help of our mentors we managed to get this team on same level regarding business intelligence, big data and data analytics. It is important to note, that this project helped the company to keep 18 experienced employees and save the cost of termination and hiring new ones.
